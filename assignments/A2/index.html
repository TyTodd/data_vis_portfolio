Dataset

This dataset tracks changes in corporate ownership and owner occupancy rates across Boston neighborhoods from 2004 to 2024, using property assessment records. Corporate ownership is identified through keyword matching in owner names, while owner occupancy is determined from assessment data. The dataset includes neighborhood-level metrics for both ownership types and integrates demographic data from the U.S. Census, such as total population, racial composition, and housing unit statistics. Additionally, a separate table maps Boston neighborhoods to zip codes for geographic reference.

Summary

Corporate landlords are highly opportunistic, expanding aggressively during economic crises like COVID-19 while individual landlords declined. Corporate ownership has risen across all Boston neighborhoods since 2004, with the sharpest spikes in wealthier, whiter areas. Meanwhile, owner occupancy rates have remained relatively stable, showing that the rise in corporate ownership isn't solely displacing homeowners but is likely pushing out small landlords instead.

The data also highlights racial disparities—corporate and individual landlords are more prevalent in white and Asian-majority neighborhoods, while Black and Hispanic communities have higher owner occupancy but lower investment ownership. This suggests a systemic imbalance in real estate access and investment opportunities. An imbalance is carried out mostly by corporate landlords but by individual landlords as well.

This supports the points brought up in the New York Times article which stated that corporate landlords are more likely to evict their tenants. Ultimately, corporate landlords treat housing purely as an investment, capitalizing on financial instability at the expense of tenant stability. These trends reinforce concerns about displacement, affordability, and the need for stronger tenant protections against corporate consolidation in the housing market. From this I think the data does support the policies proposed in the New York Times article which try to increase the number of individual landlords as opposed to corporate landlords.
